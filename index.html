Direct booking system — technical direction

The SOW (LCDM-ADM-SOW-001) is well-considered and clearly scoped. The 3-week delivery timeline is realistic for the booking module alone. Key decisions that need to be locked before build can start are flagged below.

Stack decision — booking plugin

The SOW evaluates three options. Based on the property's needs (7 rooms, seasonal pricing, channel manager to avoid double-bookings, optional activity add-ons, bilingual EN/ES), here is the prioritised view:

Recommended — MotoPress Hotel Booking (free)

Purpose-built for hospitality (not adapted e-commerce). Handles room availability, seasonal rates, deposit/balance split natively. WooCommerce handles payments only. Eliminates the integration conflict flagged in the SOW. Free tier covers the essential needs for 7 rooms. Availability sync with Booking.com via iCal feed reduces double-booking risk.

Hospitality-native logicNo WooCommerce conflictsiCal sync (not real-time)
Alternative — Lodgify (freemium widget)

The SOW notes this is not fully free but offers a free embeddable widget. Its channel manager is its strongest argument — real-time two-way sync with Booking.com/Airbnb eliminates double-booking risk entirely. For a 7-room property at this price point, the subscription cost is recouped after one or two direct bookings. Worth presenting to Adrian as the more robust commercial option.

Real-time channel syncBest conversion UXMonthly subscription
Avoid — Booking Calendar + WooCommerce

The SOW's primary option creates a known integration conflict between room availability logic (Booking Calendar) and payment flow (WooCommerce). The SOW itself identifies this risk. MotoPress solves it cleanly.

Integration riskNot hospitality-native

PR outreach strategy

The marketing plan correctly identifies PR as a key opportunity — and the property has the ingredients for genuinely compelling press coverage. The challenge is not the story; it is having the right materials and the right approach for each target market.

"A Spanish family spent ten years building a luxury lodge around a natural cave on a mountaintop in Castellón — and you've never heard of it." This is the evergreen pitch that opens doors across all markets. It is remarkable, verifiable, and deeply visual. Every press kit should lead here.

Off-grid, solar-powered, local materials, seven rooms — zero compromise on comfort. Press kit — what needs to be produced

1
Hero photography (non-negotiable) — 8–12 high-resolution images: cave entrance, pool at golden hour, terrace at sunrise, hot tub with sea view, one or two rooms, exterior from approach road, local landscape. Without this, no serious outlet will run the story.
2
One-page property fact sheet — rooms, amenities, pricing, location, booking URL. Journalists need facts fast.
3
Founder story (200 words) — the ten-year build in Adrian's own voice. This is what editors quote.
4
Three pitch letters — one per main angle, adapted per target market language (EN for UK/NL/DE, consider DE translation for German press).
5
Press contact page on website — a hidden or discreet Press section with downloadable kit, high-res images, and a direct contact. Standard expectation from any journalist who finds the property organically.

Influencer / content creator layer

The marketing plan identifies influencer partnerships as a key opportunity. The right profile for this property is niche and credible, not mass reach:

→
Eco-luxury travel creators (10k–100k, high engagement) outperform mass influencers for boutique properties. One aligned creator post outperforms ten generic ones.
→
Target creators who specifically cover sustainable travel, slow travel, or off-the-beaten-path Spain — not generic travel accounts.
→
Offer complimentary stays in exchange for content rights (usable on website and ads) — more valuable than a post alone.

Social media strategy

Your instinct — clean, aspirational, effortless, minimalist luxury — is exactly right for this property and its target audience. The references (Rosa Hotels, Omai) both demonstrate this aesthetic at its best. Below is how to operationalise it.

Visual identity direction

Aesthetic — what it should feel like

Still. Warm. Unhurried. The light of late afternoon through stone. The feeling of arriving somewhere that required effort to find. Think: wide crops with generous negative space, natural textures (rock, terracotta, linen, water), muted warm tones over high-saturation filters, human presence felt but rarely centred (a hand, a shadow, a glass on a ledge).

Omai referenceRosa Hotels referenceAvoid: over-saturated, busy, stock-feeling
Visual anchors — what to photograph repeatedly
The caveAlways the centrepiece — internal views, cave mouth at dusk, the way light enters
The poolMinimal, geometric, with mountain and sea behind — best at golden hour and blue hour
The terraceMorning coffee, evening wine, empty chairs with the view — suggest a feeling, not a product
Local texturesStone walls, olive trees, ceramic tiles, local produce — ground the property in place
The horizonSea visible from a mountain — this is the compositional surprise that differentiates the property

Platform strategy

Instagram — primary

Feed: editorial, still, wide. Stories: behind the scenes, morning light, local market. Reels: 30–60s landscape/mood clips (cave → terrace → pool → horizon). Bio: rewrite to include location + cave reference + link.

Pinterest — launch immediately

5 boards: The Lodge · The Cave · Our Rooms · Castellón Escapes · Sustainable Stays. Each pin links to the corresponding website page. Strong long-tail discovery traffic for the target persona.

Facebook — maintenance

Mirror Instagram content. Useful for the 40–55 segment and for running targeted ads to UK/NL/DE markets. Not a primary creative channel.

YouTube / Reels — phased

Commission a 90s walkthrough video (dawn-to-dusk format) in Month 2 after hero photography is done. This becomes the anchor for YouTube, Reels, and homepage embed.

Content calendar — monthly rhythm

Mon
Landscape / light — wide crop, no caption needed beyond location tag. Cave, pool, terrace, horizon. Let the image speak.
Wed
Sustainability or story — a short, honest caption about off-grid life, local sourcing, or the building process. Snami-style narrative tone: "We learned this from the mountain."
Fri
Local area or experience — Vilafamés, Desert de les Palmes, a local dish, the cave exploration, olive picking. Positions the property as a destination, not just a room.
Sun
Guest moment or testimonial — a quote from a Booking.com review, a UGC repost with permission, or a single line from a guest message. No need for a photo every time — text on a textured background works well.

UGC strategy

1
Print a small card placed in each room: "The cave tends to stay with people. If it stays with you too, share it: #LaCuevaDeMiravet"
2
Add a gentle check-out prompt: "We'd love to see your stay through your eyes." No pressure, no incentive required — at this positioning level, a genuine ask works better than a reward.
3
Set up a saved hashtag search and repost (with credit) the best guest content monthly — this signals community without manufacturing it.

Caption voice — what it sounds like

Do: "The cave doesn't announce itself. It waits." · "Six hours of light on the terrace. That's a Tuesday." · "Some mornings, the sea is closer than the road."
Don't: "Book your stay now! 🌟" · "Luxury awaits 🏔✨" · "Our amazing team welcomes you!" · Emojis leading captions.
Tone model: Snami Travel's copy — evocative, second-person, quiet confidence. Not a hotel posting; a place speaking.

Design system — recommended direction

TypographySerif display (Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond) for headings + clean sans (Inter or DM Sans) for body. The contrast signals luxury without being ornate.
Colour paletteWarm off-white base (#FDFCF8) · Deep terracotta accent (#C4622D) · Stone/olive green secondary (#5A7A6C) · Black for type. Nomada orange as a discreet branded accent.
Image treatmentFull-bleed, natural light, warm grade. No overlaid graphics. Architecture as the subject, not lifestyle models.
SpacingGenerous. Luxury is communicated through what is not on the page as much as what is.
MotionSubtle fade-in on scroll. Video hero loops silently. No bouncing animations or aggressive transitions.
